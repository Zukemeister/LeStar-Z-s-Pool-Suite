LeStar Z's Banker

A bank shot sends the object ball off a rail and into a pocket. This app calculates the geometry of that shot — where the ball needs to hit the rail, what angle you need to cut it, and how speed and spin change the result. Like all LeStar Z apps, it's a learning tool. Use it at the table to train your eye, then take that knowledge into the game.

THE TABLE (top diagram): Shows a bird's-eye view of the table. Two balls are shown — white (cue ball) and red (object ball). Drag each one to match the actual positions on your table. Tap any of the six pockets to select your target. The app instantly draws the shot:

· Gold dashed line — your approach: where the cue ball needs to travel to contact the object ball.
· Blue line — the object ball's path from your cue ball contact to the first rail.
· Green dashed line — the bank: object ball path from rail to pocket.
· White dashed circle — the ghost ball. This is the exact spot your cue ball center must pass through to send the object ball in the right direction. Aim for this.
· White ring on rail — the contact point on the rail. The diamond readout tells you where this falls in the standard diamond system.

RAIL TABS: The colored buttons below the table show all valid rails for this bank. TOP, BTM, LFT, RGT. Tap one to switch. Some positions only have one valid option; others have two or three. In 2-CUSHION mode, tabs show rail pairs (e.g. TOP→LFT).

THE AIMING DIAGRAM (bottom diagram): This is the Aimer view — a close-up of the object ball as you see it from behind the cue ball. The red ball is on the right. Your cue ball slides in from the left. The orange line (A) is the equator of the object ball — your reference line. The orange marker shows the inside edge of the cue ball at the required contact point. The percentage badge shows how much of the object ball you are hitting. The cut angle is shown top-right.

SPEED: Faster shots compress the rail more, producing a shallower rebound angle. Slower shots rebound steeper. Match this to your intended shot power and the bank geometry shifts accordingly.

SPIN: Side english on the cue ball does two things. First, it throws the object ball slightly off the pure geometric line — the diagram adjusts the aim point automatically. Second, the object ball arrives at the rail with its own running english from the cut, which opens or closes the rebound. The THROW ADJUSTMENT readout tells you how much to compensate.

BIAS: Every table's rails play a little differently. If your banks consistently go long (past the pocket), push BIAS toward SHORT. If they come up short, push toward LONG. Dial this in over a practice session and it will make the whole app more accurate for your specific table.

1 CUSHION / 2 CUSHIONS: Most banks use one rail. Occasionally the geometry requires two. Tap 2 CUSHIONS to see all valid two-rail routes — the app computes them using the same compressed-reflection physics, just chained twice. Two-cushion banks are harder to execute but sometimes the only option.

DIAMONDS: The standard reference system. 0 at each corner, 4 at the side pockets on the long rails, 1–3 in between. A readout of TOP ◆ 2.50 means the contact point is exactly halfway between diamonds 2 and 3 on the top rail.

ROTATE ⟳: Cycles the table through 0° / 90° / 180° / 270°. Turn it to match how you're standing at the table. Dragging works correctly at every orientation.
